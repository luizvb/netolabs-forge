# Go-to-market: Qualification + Scheduling Kit

Status: commercial hypothesis for design-partner validation
Date: 2026-07-15

## The pain worth buying

High-ticket service companies lose revenue between first contact and the first useful conversation. Leads arrive outside business hours, wait for a reply, repeat basic context to an operator, exchange messages to find a time and disappear before a diagnostic is booked. The manager usually cannot distinguish low demand from slow response, poor fit or scheduling friction.

The Kit sells a measurable operational outcome: a qualified meeting confirmed from a public intake journey. It should not be positioned as “an AI chatbot”. The buyer receives a defined qualification contract, controlled availability, duplicate-booking protection, a public link, regression tests and an outcome dashboard.

## Initial customer profile

Prioritize companies with all of these signals:

- An average sale large enough that one additional win can pay for several months of the service.
- At least 30 inbound opportunities per month or meaningful after-hours lead volume.
- A repeatable first diagnostic, visit, consultation or commercial meeting.
- A manager who can define service area, fit criteria and weekly availability.
- A current journey based on forms, direct messages or manual back-and-forth scheduling.

Start with one narrow vertical and repeat its language and proof. Solar installers and B2B consultancies are strong design-partner candidates because fit, territory, decision role, timing and investment readiness are natural qualification inputs. Regulated advice, clinical triage and autonomous contract approval remain excluded.

## Offer structure

### 1. Paid design-partner pilot

- Discovery and funnel baseline.
- Kit configuration and branded public link.
- Qualification criteria and internal availability setup.
- Five safety/behavior evals and launch review.
- 30-day operation with weekly outcome review.
- End report: starts, completions, qualified leads, bookings, conflicts and operator feedback.

Pricing hypothesis, not a market claim: `R$ 1.500–3.000` implementation plus `R$ 750–1.500` for the first operated month. Discount only in exchange for weekly access, anonymized outcome evidence and permission to build a case study if the pilot succeeds.

### 2. Recurring managed Kit

Pricing hypothesis: `R$ 997–2.497/month` by lead volume, support level and integrations, with implementation charged separately. Keep model tokens and channel/provider fees inside a documented fair-use boundary until enough usage evidence exists for a stable allowance.

### 3. Expansion revenue

Sell capabilities after the core outcome is proven, in this order:

1. Google Calendar or Calendly reconciliation.
2. CRM write-back and pipeline attribution.
3. WhatsApp channel and human handoff.
4. Quote/proposal generation with approval.
5. Reactivation and renewal journeys.

Do not create a different “agent” for every connector. The commercial unit is the outcome Kit; connectors raise reliability, adoption and account value.

## Buyer-facing promise and boundaries

Suggested promise:

> Transform inbound interest into qualified, scheduled conversations with a controlled journey your team can audit.

Commit only to what Forge controls: response availability, question flow, scoring rules, offered internal slots, confirmed internal bookings and reporting. Do not promise sales conversion, external-calendar blocking, WhatsApp delivery or CRM synchronization before those adapters are installed and verified.

## Sales motion

### Discovery

Ask for the last 30 days:

- How many leads arrived, through which channels and at what times?
- How long until the first useful response?
- How many became qualified conversations and confirmed meetings?
- Where does the team repeat work or lose context?
- What makes a lead eligible, and who can change that rule?
- What is the gross profit from an average closed deal?

Disqualify accounts without a repeatable meeting outcome, an accountable owner or enough lead flow to observe a result.

### Demo

Use the buyer's offer, territory and meeting title. Show the consent screen, one qualification path, a valid booking, a conflicting booking and the operator dashboard. The conflict demonstration is important: it proves the product is an operational system, not a scripted conversation.

### Business case

Use a simple break-even model:

`required additional closed deals = monthly Kit cost / gross profit per closed deal`

Then expose the uncertain conversion chain rather than hiding it:

`leads × completion rate × qualification rate × booking rate × show rate × close rate`

The pilot succeeds when it produces verified bookings, reduces manual scheduling work and gives the manager a trustworthy funnel baseline. Closed revenue is a later lagging signal influenced by the sales team.

## Delivery journey

| Stage | Owner | Output | Exit gate |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Diagnose | Commercial + operator | Baseline, fit rules, exclusions and target meeting | One accountable owner approves the contract |
| Configure | Implementation | Installed Kit, availability and five evals | Operator reviews rules and test cases |
| Validate | Implementation + operator | Happy path, disqualification, resume and booking-conflict evidence | No critical privacy/action failure |
| Publish | Operator | Public link placed in one approved channel | Link is active and human recovery is named |
| Operate | Operator | Lead follow-up and weekly outcome review | Every booking has a clear human owner |
| Learn | Product + commercial | Funnel report, objections and requested integrations | Continue, change or stop decision at day 30 |
| Expand | Account owner | Calendar/CRM/channel capability | Core qualified-booking outcome is already proven |

## Pilot scorecard

- North star: qualified bookings per active agent per week.
- Funnel: started, completed, qualified, booked and booking conflicts.
- Operational: median time to booking, manual touches avoided and follow-up SLA.
- Quality: invalid fit decisions, duplicate bookings, privacy incidents and operator overrides.
- Commercial: pilot-to-recurring conversion, expansion request and payback hypothesis.

Stop or redesign the offer when fewer than 3 of 5 design partners produce one verified booking within seven days of configuration, operators repeatedly override the fit contract, or buyers value only a generic chat experience rather than the measurable booking outcome.

## Common objections

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| “We already have a form.” | A form collects fields; this Kit also applies a controlled fit contract, offers valid availability, prevents duplicates and reports the resulting funnel. |
| “We already use Calendly.” | Calendly solves a calendar step. The Kit adds qualification and outcome evidence; external calendar reconciliation is the next adapter, not a competing agent. |
| “AI may promise the wrong thing.” | Public qualification and booking are deterministic. The model cannot invent eligibility or a confirmed time. |
| “What if the lead needs a person?” | The journey has a named operator owner and safe terminal states; channel-level handoff is an explicit expansion capability. |
| “How do we prove ROI?” | Establish the current funnel, measure verified qualified bookings and manual effort during the paid pilot, then compare break-even against gross profit per closed deal. |

## Evidence to collect before scaling

- Five completed design-partner pilots in one vertical.
- At least three verified booking outcomes within the first week of launch.
- Operator interviews on false qualification, no-show and follow-up ownership.
- Distribution of lead volume and booking conflicts to price fair-use limits.
- Which calendar and CRM providers are actually requested, rather than assumed.
- One permissioned case study with baseline, intervention, funnel change and limitations.
